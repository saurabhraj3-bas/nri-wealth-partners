## 2. Determining Your Tax Residency Status

### India Tax Residency Rules (FY 2024-25)

**Resident:**
- Present in India for 182 days or more during the financial year, OR
- Present for 60 days or more during the year AND 365 days or more in preceding 4 years

**Non-Resident (NRI):**
- Does NOT meet the above criteria

**Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident (RNOR):**
- Resident in India but NRI in 9 out of 10 preceding years, OR
- Resident but present in India for 729 days or less in preceding 7 years

**Important Changes (Budget 2025):**
- Deemed residency for Indian citizens with income >INR 15 lakhs if not paying tax in any country
- Special provisions for Indian students and workers abroad

### Decision Tree: Am I an NRI?

```
Were you in India for 182+ days in FY 2024-25?
  ↓ No
Were you in India for 60+ days in FY 2024-25 AND 365+ days in preceding 4 years?
  ↓ No
YOU ARE AN NRI ✅
  ↓ Yes (to either)
YOU ARE A RESIDENT (may qualify for RNOR)
```

## 3. India Tax Filing Requirements for NRIs

### What Income is Taxable in India?

**Taxable in India:**
✅ Income earned or received in India
✅ Income from business controlled from India
✅ Salary for services rendered in India
✅ Rental income from Indian property
✅ Capital gains on sale of Indian assets
✅ Interest from NRO accounts
✅ Dividends from Indian companies

**NOT Taxable in India (for NRIs):**
❌ Interest from NRE accounts (exempt)
❌ Interest from FCNR accounts (exempt)
❌ Foreign salary (for services outside India)
❌ Foreign income not received in India

### Tax Rates for NRIs (FY 2024-25)

**Old Tax Regime:**
| Income Slab (INR) | Tax Rate |
|-------------------|----------|
| Up to 2,50,000 | Nil |
| 2,50,001 to 5,00,000 | 5% |
| 5,00,001 to 10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above 10,00,000 | 30% |

**New Tax Regime (Recommended for most NRIs):**
| Income Slab (INR) | Tax Rate |
|-------------------|----------|
| Up to 3,00,000 | Nil |
| 3,00,001 to 6,00,000 | 5% |
| 6,00,001 to 9,00,000 | 10% |
| 9,00,001 to 12,00,000 | 15% |
| 12,00,001 to 15,00,000 | 20% |
| Above 15,00,000 | 30% |

**Note:** NRIs do NOT get Section 80C, 80D deductions under new regime.

### ITR Forms for NRIs

**ITR-1 (Sahaj):**
- For salary, one house property, other sources
- Income up to INR 50 lakhs
- NOT for NRIs with foreign assets

**ITR-2:**
- Most common for NRIs
- Salary, house property, capital gains
- Covers foreign income and assets

**ITR-3:**
- For NRIs with business/profession income

### Step-by-Step: Filing ITR for NRIs

**Step 1: Gather Documents**
- Form 16 (if applicable)
- Bank statements (NRO, NRE accounts)
- Interest certificates from banks
- Property tax receipts
- Capital gains statements
- TDS certificates (Form 26AS)
- Foreign income details (for RNOR)

**Step 2: Calculate Taxable Income**
- Add all India-sourced income
- Deduct applicable deductions (old regime only)
- Check TDS already deducted

**Step 3: Choose Tax Regime**
- Old regime: More deductions, higher rates
- New regime: Lower rates, fewer deductions
- Use online calculator to compare

**Step 4: File Online via Income Tax Portal**
1. Login to www.incometax.gov.in
2. Select appropriate ITR form
3. Fill in personal details
4. Enter income details from each source
5. Claim DTAA relief if applicable
6. Review and submit
7. E-verify using:
   - Aadhaar OTP
   - Net banking
   - Digital signature
   - EVC (Electronic Verification Code)

**Step 5: Pay Tax (if due)**
- Use Challan 280 on income tax portal
- Payment via net banking/debit card
- Keep challan copy for records

## 4. US Tax Filing for Indian NRIs

### US Tax Residency

**US Citizen or Green Card Holder:**
- Taxed on worldwide income regardless of residence
- MUST file US tax return

**Substantial Presence Test (for visa holders):**
- Present in US for 183+ days in current year, OR
- Formula: (Days in current year) + (Days in prior year ÷ 3) + (Days in 2nd prior year ÷ 6) ≥ 183

**Example:** H1B holder present 300 days in 2024, 200 days in 2023, 100 days in 2022:
- 300 + (200÷3) + (100÷6) = 300 + 67 + 17 = 384 days
- **Result:** US Resident for tax purposes

### Key US Tax Forms

**Form 1040:** US Individual Income Tax Return
**Schedule B:** Interest and Dividend Income
**Form 8938:** Foreign Financial Assets (if >$50K)
**FinCEN Form 114 (FBAR):** Foreign Bank Accounts (if >$10K)
**Form 2555:** Foreign Earned Income Exclusion
**Form 1116:** Foreign Tax Credit

### FBAR Reporting (Critical!)

**Who Must File:**
- US persons with foreign accounts totaling >$10,000 at ANY time during the year

**What to Report:**
- Bank accounts in India (NRE, NRO, savings)
- Investment accounts
- Mutual funds
- Joint accounts (full value)

**Deadline:** April 15 (auto-extension to October 15)

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**
- Unintentional: Up to $10,000 per violation
- Willful: Greater of $100,000 or 50% of account balance

### FATCA Form 8938

**Who Must File:**
- US residents with foreign assets >$50,000 on last day OR >$75,000 anytime during year
- Filing with spouse: $100,000 on last day OR $150,000 anytime

**Penalties:** $10,000 for non-filing, additional $10,000 every 30 days (max $50,000)

### US-India Tax Treaty Benefits

**Article 21 - Other Income:**
- Items not specifically mentioned taxed only in residence country

**Article 24 - Relief from Double Taxation:**
- US allows Foreign Tax Credit for Indian taxes paid
- India allows credit for US taxes paid

**How to Claim:**
- Use Form 1116 for Foreign Tax Credit
- Attach tax payment proof from India
- Reduces US tax dollar-for-dollar (within limits)

## 5. UK Tax Filing for Indian NRIs

### UK Tax Residency (Statutory Residence Test)

**Automatic UK Resident if:**
- Spend 183+ days in UK in tax year, OR
- Only home is in UK, OR
- Work full-time in UK

**Automatic Non-Resident if:**
- Spend <16 days in UK (or <46 days if not UK resident in prior 3 years)

**Sufficient Ties Test:**
- Family, accommodation, work, 90-day ties
- Complex calculation - consult advisor if unclear

### Remittance Basis for NRIs

**What is Remittance Basis?**
- Pay UK tax only on UK income and foreign income brought to UK
- Must claim annually
- Lose personal allowance if used

**When to Use:**
- High foreign income not needed in UK
- Can leave funds offshore

**When NOT to Use:**
- Foreign income is low
- Need to remit most foreign income to UK

### Self Assessment Tax Return

**Who Must File:**
- Self-employed
- Income >£100,000
- Capital gains above annual exemption
- Foreign income >£2,000

**Deadline:**
- Online filing: January 31 following tax year
- Paper filing: October 31

### UK-India DTAA

**Relief Methods:**
- Credit method (more common)
- Exemption method

**Commonly Used Articles:**
- Article 11: Interest income
- Article 15: Employment income
- Article 23: Relief from double taxation

## 6. Canada Tax Filing for Indian NRIs

### Canada Tax Residency

**Factual Resident:**
- Maintain significant residential ties (home, spouse, dependents)
- Taxed on worldwide income

**Non-Resident:**
- Severed residential ties
- Taxed only on Canadian-source income

**Deemed Non-Resident:**
- Factual resident but treaty makes you non-resident of Canada

### Filing T1 General Return

**Deadline:** April 30 (June 15 if self-employed)

**Common Forms:**
- T1135: Foreign Income Verification (if foreign property >CAD 100,000)
- T2209: Federal Foreign Tax Credits
- Schedule 3: Capital Gains/Losses

### Canada-India DTAA

**Tie-Breaker Rules:**
1. Permanent home
2. Centre of vital interests
3. Habitual abode
4. Nationality
5. Mutual agreement

## 7. UAE & Middle East Considerations

### Tax-Free Income

**Good News:** UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar have NO personal income tax

**BUT... India Still Taxes:**
- UAE salary is EXEMPT in India for NRIs (since earned outside India)
- HOWEVER, if you qualify as Indian resident, worldwide income is taxable

**Deemed Residency Risk:**
- Indian citizens with income >INR 15 lakhs
- Not paying tax in any country
- Could be deemed resident in India

**Solution:**
- Maintain NRI status (stay <182 days in India)
- Keep proof of UAE residence (visa, employment contract, rent agreement)

### Tax Residency Certificate (TRC)

**What is TRC?**
- Certificate from tax authority proving you're resident there
- Needed to claim DTAA benefits

**UAE TRC:**
- Can obtain from Federal Tax Authority
- Required for claiming DTAA benefits in India

## 8. Double Taxation Avoidance Agreements (DTAA)

### What is DTAA?

Treaty between two countries to:
- Decide which country has right to tax
- Provide relief if both countries tax same income
- Prevent tax evasion

### India has DTAA with 90+ Countries

**Major Countries:**
- USA, UK, Canada, Australia
- Singapore, UAE, Saudi Arabia
- Germany, France, Netherlands
- Japan, South Korea, China

### How to Claim DTAA Benefits in India

**For Lower TDS Rates:**

**Step 1:** Obtain Tax Residency Certificate from foreign country

**Step 2:** Submit Form 10F to Indian payer
- Details: Name, address, TIN, residency period
- Attach TRC copy

**Step 3:** Payer deducts TDS at treaty rate (instead of 30%)

**Example:**
- Dividend from Indian company to US resident
- Normal TDS: 20%
- Treaty rate (Article 10): 15%
- **Savings: 5% upfront**

**For Tax Return Filing:**

**Step 1:** Pay full Indian tax initially

**Step 2:** File ITR and claim foreign tax credit in residence country

**Example:**
- Rental income from India: INR 5,00,000
- Indian tax paid: INR 70,000
- Report in US return, claim $900 foreign tax credit
- Reduces US tax liability

### Lower TDS Rates Under Common DTAAs

| Country | Interest | Dividend | Royalty | Technical Fees |
|---------|----------|----------|---------|----------------|
| USA | 15% | 15-25% | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| UK | 15% | 15% | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| Canada | 15% | 15-25% | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| Singapore | 15% | 10% | 10% | 10% |
| UAE | 12.5% | 10% | 10% | 10% |

## 9. Common Tax Filing Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake #1: Not Filing Because "No Tax Due"

**Reality:** Must file if income > basic exemption limit, even if no tax due (after TDS)

**Consequence:** Notice from income tax department, potential penalty

**Solution:** File NIL return or claim refund

### ❌ Mistake #2: Claiming Deductions Not Allowed for NRIs

**Not Allowed:**
- Section 80C (under new regime)
- HRA exemption (NRIs don't get HRA from Indian employers typically)

**Allowed:**
- Section 80TTA: NRE account interest (exempt anyway)
- Home loan interest deduction (under old regime)

### ❌ Mistake #3: Not Reporting Foreign Assets in India Tax Return

**Requirement (Schedule FA):**
- Must report foreign bank accounts, property, assets
- Even if no income generated

**Penalty:** INR 10 lakhs for willful non-disclosure

### ❌ Mistake #4: Wrong Tax Regime Selection

**Problem:** Choosing old regime without deductions available

**Solution:** Use online calculator, compare both regimes

### ❌ Mistake #5: Missing FBAR Deadline

**Consequence:** Penalty up to $100,000 or 50% of account

**Solution:** Set calendar reminder for April 15, or hire professional

### ❌ Mistake #6: Not Keeping Tax Payment Proof

**Why Needed:**
- Claim foreign tax credit
- Prove DTAA claim
- Future visa applications

**Solution:** Save all:
- Tax returns (7 years)
- Payment challans
- TDS certificates
- Bank statements

### ❌ Mistake #7: Ignoring State Taxes (USA)

**Reality:** 43 US states have income tax (varying rates)

**Forget:** California 13.3%, New York 10.9%, Texas 0%

**Solution:** File both federal and state returns

## 11. Tax-Saving Strategies for NRIs

### Strategy #1: Optimize Account Types

**NRE Account:**
- Interest is tax-free
- Use for parking foreign earnings
- Fully repatriable

**NRO Account:**
- Interest taxable @30% TDS
- Use only for India-sourced income
- Limited repatriation (USD 1M/year)

**Recommendation:** Keep maximum in NRE accounts

### Strategy #2: Home Loan Interest Deduction

**Old Tax Regime:**
- Deduct up to INR 2,00,000 interest per year
- Self-occupied: Section 24(b)
- Let-out property: No limit

**Strategy:**
- If you have home loan, calculate old vs new regime
- Old regime may save more tax

### Strategy #3: Claim Foreign Tax Credit

**Don't Pay Tax Twice!**
- If you paid tax in India on rental income
- Claim credit in US/UK/Canada return
- Reduces effective tax rate

**Example:**
- India rental income: INR 10,00,000
- Indian tax: INR 2,00,000 (20%)
- US tax rate: 24%
- Foreign tax credit: $2,400
- Net US tax: $240 (on $10,000 income @24% = $2,400 - $2,400 credit)

### Strategy #4: Time Your Repatriation

**Scenario:** Selling Indian property

**Bad Timing:**
- Sell in March 2025
- Repatriate in April 2025
- Pay capital gains tax in India in July 2025 (ITR filing)
- Can't claim credit in US 2024 return (filed April 2025)
- Must amend 2025 return later

**Good Timing:**
- Sell in April 2025
- Pay capital gains in India by July 2026
- Claim foreign tax credit in US 2025 return (filed April 2026)
- Everything aligns

### Strategy #5: Utilize Capital Gains Exemptions

**Section 54 (India):**
- Sell residential property
- Invest in another residential property within 2 years
- Capital gains exempt (up to investment amount)

**Works for NRIs!** Can claim even if you're abroad

**Section 54EC:**
- Invest capital gains in specified bonds (NHAI, REC)
- ₹50 lakh limit
- Lock-in: 5 years

### Strategy #6: Gift Strategically

**India Gift Tax Rules:**
- Gifts to relatives: Tax-free (parents, siblings, spouse)
- Gifts to others: >₹50,000 taxable to recipient

**Strategy:**
- Parent in India wants to buy property
- NRI gifts ₹50 lakhs to parent (tax-free)
- Parent buys property
- Property in parent's name (not yours)
- Avoids NRI repatriation hassles, potential future capital gains in your return

**Caution:** Genuinely gift, don't maintain control

### Strategy #7: Choose Right Investment Structure

**Direct Stocks:**
- Capital gains: 10% LTCG (>INR 1L), 15% STCG
- Dividends: 20% TDS for NRIs

**Mutual Funds:**
- Equity: 10% LTCG, 15% STCG
- Debt: 20% LTCG (with indexation), 30% STCG

**NRE/FCNR FD:**
- Interest tax-free
- Low returns (6-7%)

**Recommendation for Tax Efficiency:**
- NRE FD for emergency funds (tax-free)
- Equity mutual funds for growth (lower tax rates)
- Direct stocks if you're active investor

## 13. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### Q1: I'm an NRI. Do I need to file tax in India if I have no income?

**A:** No, if you have absolutely no India-sourced income and no TDS was deducted, filing is not mandatory. However, it's good practice to file NIL return to maintain clean records.

### Q2: Can I file India tax return from abroad?

**A:** Yes! File online at www.incometax.gov.in. E-verify using Aadhaar OTP (if Aadhaar is linked to mobile), net banking, or generate EVC.

### Q3: I forgot to file FBAR. What should I do?

**A:** File immediately using the Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedures if unintentional. Consult a US tax professional. Do NOT ignore it.

### Q4: How do I get a Tax Residency Certificate?

**US:** File Form 8802 with IRS (6-8 weeks processing)
**UK:** Apply online through HMRC
**Canada:** Available through CRA My Account
**UAE:** Apply via Federal Tax Authority portal

### Q5: Can I claim both Section 54 exemption and foreign tax credit?

**A:** Yes, they're independent. Section 54 exempts Indian capital gains. Foreign tax credit reduces tax in your residence country.

### Q6: I work remotely for Indian company from USA. Where do I pay tax?

**A:** Depends on physical location of work. If you physically work in USA (even remotely), it's US-sourced income. Taxable in US. May also be taxable in India if you're Indian resident. Check US-India treaty Article 15.

### Q7: What if my country's tax return deadline is before India's?

**A:** File your country's return by their deadline. Estimate India taxes if not yet paid. Amend later if needed, or claim foreign tax credit in next year's return.

### Q8: Are NRE account returns taxable in my residence country?

**A:** Depends on country. USA, UK, Canada tax worldwide income (including NRE interest). UAE doesn't. Even though exempt in India, you must report it in your residence country.

### Q9: Can I use Indian CA to file my US taxes?

**A:** Only if they're licensed (CPA, Enrolled Agent). Most Indian CAs aren't. Hire US-based tax professional or EA specializing in expat taxes.

### Q10: I'm moving back to India. How to handle taxes?

**A:** Complex! You may be resident of BOTH countries in transition year. File in both countries, use treaty tie-breaker rules, claim foreign tax credit. Consult cross-border tax advisor 3-6 months BEFORE moving.
